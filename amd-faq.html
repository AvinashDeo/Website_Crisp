FAQ

  1. What are the first sign of macular degeneration?
    Early signs of vision loss from AMD include shadowy areas in your central vision or unusually fuzzy or distorted vision.
  2. What causes age related macular degeneration?
    The exact cause of AMD is not known, but it has been linked to a number of risk factors. These include having excess weight and high blood pressure, smoking, and having a family history of the condition
  3. What age does macular degeneration begin?
    Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) is an eye disease that may get worse over time. It's the leading cause of severe, permanent vision loss in people over age 60. It happens when the small central portion of your retina, called the macula, wears down.
